PPTX Editor in Your Browser

Edit PowerPoint presentations locally in your browser without uploading PPTX files to a document server. Your slides never leave your device.

What You Can Do

  • Open .pptx presentation files directly from your computer
  • Edit slide text, images, shapes, transitions, and speaker notes
  • Create new presentations from scratch and save as PPTX
  • Preview slides in a full-screen view before presenting
  • Load a PPTX from a remote URL using the ?src= parameter

Why Edit Presentations Without Uploading?

Pitch decks, internal strategy presentations, and client proposals often contain confidential business information. When you upload a PPTX to an online tool, that content passes through an external server. ByBrowser processes your presentation entirely in the browser using WebAssembly — slides are never transmitted to any server, and no account is required.

How to Open a PowerPoint File

  1. Click the upload button or drag and drop your .pptx file onto the editor
  2. The presentation opens using the OnlyOffice engine — completely locally
  3. Edit slides, rearrange content, and adjust formatting
  4. Save the finished presentation back to your device as a PPTX file

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I edit PPTX files without Microsoft PowerPoint?
Yes. The OnlyOffice presentation engine handles PPTX files with slide layouts, animations, and embedded media — no PowerPoint required.
Are slide transitions and animations preserved?
Yes. Transitions and standard animations defined in the PPTX format are supported and preserved on save.
Can I present directly from the browser?
You can view slides in the editor's full-page view. Full presentation mode depends on browser capabilities.
Is there a limit on the number of slides?
No hard limit is imposed by the tool — very large presentations may be slower to load depending on available browser memory.
